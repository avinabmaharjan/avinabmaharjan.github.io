Skills · In progress

What I’m learning to master.

I don’t believe in "I already know this." Everything here is a living process — built from curiosity, repetition, frustration, and small breakthroughs.

Networking

Seeing how everything connects

I’m learning how data really moves — from cables and packets to protocols and routing. I break down abstract concepts into simple mental models I can test in small labs and simulations.

Approach: I learn by tracing paths, drawing diagrams, and asking "What actually happens under the hood?" until it stops being magic.

Cybersecurity

Curious, careful, ethical

Security, to me, is about responsibility. I’m exploring how systems break, how attackers think, and how defenders can protect what matters — while respecting boundaries and ethics.

Approach: I learn by experimenting in safe, isolated environments, reading real-world incident stories, and asking, "How could this have been prevented?"

Automation & Scripting

Making the boring parts lighter

I write small scripts to remove friction from my day: repetitive checks, file operations, cleanup tasks, and anything that makes my future self’s life easier.

Approach: I start with a tiny annoyance and ask, "Can code do this for me?" Then I iterate, refactor, and try to make each script a little clearer than the last.

Business Experiments

Ideas, tested in the real world

I like to turn vague ideas into small, low-risk experiments — tiny launches, quick landing pages, or prototypes that answer simple questions like "Does anyone actually want this?".

Approach: I learn by shipping imperfect things, watching how people react, and treating every result — success or flop — as data, not a verdict on my worth.

Creative Projects

Making room for soul

Beyond technical work, I care about how things feel. I enjoy crafting small, thoughtful experiences — interfaces, stories, visualizations — that help people see something familiar in a new way.

Approach: I learn by following my curiosity, letting myself play, and accepting that not everything I make has to be "useful" to be meaningful.

Projects · Small steps

Things I’ve built to learn.

These aren’t massive products. They’re honest attempts to solve my own problems, explore ideas, and practice finishing what I start.

To-Do App

Helps me remember what I forget.

A simple task manager I built for myself when sticky notes and scattered phone reminders stopped working.

Problem I was solving: I kept losing track of small but important tasks. I wanted a calm, minimal space to capture what mattered without overwhelming me.

What I learned: How even tiny UX details — like default states, clear priorities, and gentle reminders — can make a tool feel like a partner instead of a nag. I also learned that building a "basic" app is still real practice and worth being proud of.

Security Checker CLI

A small guard dog for my own systems.

A command-line tool that runs basic checks on local configurations and highlights potential weak spots.

Problem I was solving: I wanted a quick way to scan my setup, practice thinking like an attacker and a defender, and create something that made security feel more tangible than just theory.

What I learned: How to structure CLI tools, parse output cleanly, and prioritize which checks matter most. More importantly, I learned that security is less about paranoia and more about building thoughtful, layered habits.

Social Media Map

Seeing the scroll from above.

A data visualization project that maps how time, posts, or topics cluster across my social media usage.

Problem I was solving: I wanted to confront how I actually spend my attention — not how I think I do. Turning data into visuals made my habits impossible to ignore.

What I learned: How powerful visual feedback can be in changing behavior. I also got better at working with APIs, cleaning data, and designing visuals that told a story without shouting.
